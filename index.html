Overview

Our V2V communication system is designed to significantly enhance road safety by enabling real-time information exchange between vehicles. This system is developed using embedded Linux, leveraging a Yocto-built image on a Raspberry Pi platform. Employing the MVC (Model-View-Controller) design pattern, our software architecture ensures modularity, scalability, and ease of maintenance.

The hardware design integrates multiple advanced sensors and components, including ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection, GPS for precise geolocation, an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) for orientation and motion tracking, encoders for accurate rotational position and speed measurement, an STM32F401 microcontroller for precise motion control, and an ESP32 module utilizing the ESP-NOW communication protocol for efficient and reliable wireless communication.

Features

Don't Pass Warning (DNPW)

Provides alerts to prevent passing other vehicles when it's unsafe, enhancing overall road safety.

Blind Spot Warning (BSW)

Alerts drivers when a vehicle is detected in their blind spot, reducing the risk of accidents during lane changes.

Forward Collision Warning (FCW)

Warns drivers of potential collisions with vehicles or obstacles ahead, allowing for timely action to avoid accidents.

Emergency Electronic Brake Light (EEBL)

Signals to following vehicles when the brakes are applied sharply, improving reaction time and preventing rear-end collisions.

Intersection Movement Assist (IMA)

Helps drivers make safe maneuvers at intersections by providing alerts about cross traffic and potential hazards.

Hardware

The project includes the following hardware components:

  • Ultrasonic Sensors
  • GPS
  • IMU
  • Encoder
  • STM32F401 for Motion
  • ESP32 for ESP-NOW Communication

Software

The software is built using embedded Linux on a Raspberry Pi, with a Yocto image and the MVC design pattern.
